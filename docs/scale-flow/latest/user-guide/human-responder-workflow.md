# Human Responder Workflow

Human responders are staff members who talk to customers, solve issues, and take over when [AI Assistant](./ai-assistant) should not continue alone.

AI helps with speed, but humans handle judgment, empathy, exceptions, and final decisions.

## What human responders do

Human responders:

- Reply to customers in [Inbox](./inbox-usage).
- Review customer details before answering.
- Use AI summaries or writing help when useful.
- Create and update [Tickets](./ticket-usage).
- Transfer conversations to the right teammate or team.
- Close conversations when the customer is helped.

Example: AI answers a product question automatically. The customer then complains about a damaged item. A human responder takes over, apologizes, creates a ticket, and coordinates a replacement.

## Daily workflow

1. Sign in and set status to **Available**.
2. Open [Inbox](./inbox-usage).
3. Start with **Assigned to me** or your team queue.
4. Review customer profile, conversation history, and tags.
5. Check whether AI has already replied or summarized the case.
4. Respond, clarify, and resolve the issue.
6. Create a ticket if follow-up is needed.
7. Close or transfer the conversation when the next step is clear.

## Inbox queues

- **Assigned to me**: active conversations owned by the current agent.
- **Team queue**: unassigned conversations waiting for pickup.
- **Mentions**: conversations where someone asked for your help.
- **Unassigned**: conversations that need an owner.

## Response standards

- Reply clearly and politely.
- Confirm what the customer needs before giving a solution.
- Use approved information from Knowledge or company policy.
- Add internal notes before transferring a case.
- Do not promise refunds, timelines, or exceptions unless approved.

## Collaboration rules

1. Use internal mention comments when requesting help.
2. Reassign with a clear reason and next action.
3. Check the conversation before replying so two people do not answer at the same time.
4. Inform customers when ownership changes.

## When to take over from AI

Take over when:

- The customer is upset.
- The case involves money, refunds, legal concerns, or private information.
- AI says it does not know the answer.
- The customer asks for a human.
- A ticket needs investigation.
- The situation requires judgment or approval.

## Real-world workflow

1. Customer sends a Facebook message: "I want to cancel my order."
2. Smart Assistant asks for the order number.
3. The customer says they were charged twice.
4. Smart Assistant creates a ticket and transfers the conversation.
5. A human responder reads the Smart Summary.
6. The responder checks the order, adds an internal note, and updates the ticket.
7. The responder replies to the customer with the confirmed next step.

## Best practices

- Keep messages short, clear, and action-oriented.
- Tag root cause and resolution type for analytics.
- Close conversations only after customer confirmation or clear closure conditions.
- Use [AI Assistant](./ai-assistant) for support, but keep humans responsible for sensitive decisions.
