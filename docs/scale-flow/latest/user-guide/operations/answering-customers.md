# Answering customers in Inbox

This guide is for **you if your job is to read customer messages and write back** inside ScaleFlow. It is not for people who only set up the system.

You will learn: where to find your chats, how the screen is laid out, how to work with **automatic AI replies** when your company turns them on, and when **you** should write instead. If you need every button explained, open **[Inbox Usage](./inbox-usage)** after this page.

## Simple words we use in ScaleFlow

- **Inbox** — The screen where all customer chats from Facebook, Zalo, WhatsApp, Telegram, and so on show up in one place (after your company connects those apps).
- **Conversation** — One back-and-forth chat with one customer.
- **Reply** — A message the **customer can read**.
- **Internal note** — A message **only people inside your company** can read. Good for “I tried this…” before you send the chat to someone else.
- **Ticket** — A small “job card” for a problem that is not finished in one message (for example a refund that needs checking). See **[Ticket Usage](./ticket-usage)**.
- **AI Assistant** — Smart helpers that can suggest text, shorten a chat into a summary, or even answer the customer **automatically** if your boss turned that on. Details: **[AI Assistant](../scaleflow-ai/ai-assistant)**.
- **Assigned to me** — Chats that are **yours** to answer right now.
- **Unassigned** — New chats **waiting for anyone** on the team to pick them up.
- **Stage / lifecycle** — A label for “where this customer is in the journey” (for example *New* or *Paying customer*). Your company chooses the names. You do not need to be an expert—just pick the stage your boss asks you to use.

## What you actually do each day

1. Open **Inbox**.
2. Open chats that are **yours**, or pick a new one from **Unassigned** if your team works that way.
3. Read the full chat from top to bottom.
4. Write a **Reply** to the customer, or add an **Internal note** for your team.
5. If the problem needs work after this chat, **create or update a ticket**.
6. If someone else should answer next, **hand the chat to them** using the controls at the top of the chat.

## Before you start

1. Sign in to ScaleFlow.
2. Click **Inbox** on the left.
3. On most days, first click **Assigned to me** so you see **your** chats.

## How the Inbox screen is split (three parts)

Think of three columns:

![Inbox overview — three columns: categories, list, conversation](/static/img/inbox.png)

- **Left** — Folders and lists such as *Assigned to me*, *Unassigned*, and your company’s stages.
- **Middle** — The list of chats. You can search and filter here when the list is long.
- **Right** — The open chat, the box where you type, and information about the customer (and tickets).

## Step 1 — Choose which list to open

Pick the list that matches what you need to do right now:

![Left panel — My inbox and other lists](/static/img/choose-list-inbox.png)

| List name | Plain meaning |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Assigned to me** | Chats you are responsible for. **Start here on a normal day.** |
| **Assigned to my team** | Chats owned by your group. Use this if your boss tells you to watch the team list. |
| **Mentions** | Chats where someone **tagged you** or asked for you. |
| **Unassigned** | New chats **nobody owns yet**. Open one when you are free and your process says you can “pick up” work. |
| **All** | Everything. Often long; your boss may use it more than you. |

If there are too many rows, use the search box and filters:

![Search, sort, and filters on the conversation list](/static/img/filter-inbox.png)

## Step 2 — Open one chat and read everything

1. Click one row in the middle list.
2. Scroll up and down in the chat. Check if **a computer (AI) already answered**. Those messages are still worth reading—sometimes they need a human fix.
3. Look at the **right side** for the customer’s name, tags, stage, and any **Tickets** already open.

![Contact and tickets on the right panel](/static/img/profile-inbox.png)

## Step 3 — Write to the customer or to your team only

- **Reply** tab = goes to the customer.
- **Internal note** tab = your team only. Use it before you pass the chat on, so the next person knows what you did.

![Reply tab vs Internal note tab](/static/img/reply_internal_note.png)

**Before you press Send on a Reply, ask yourself:**

- Do I understand what they want?
- Am I using **true company information** (from training, internal docs, or approved answers)? If you are guessing, say you will check or hand to a specialist.
- If something is unclear, is **one short question** better than a long wrong answer?

## Step 4 — When the computer (AI) helps you

Your company may turn on tools that **write drafts** or **answer the customer without you**. You are still responsible for what the customer reads.

![AI help near the box where you type](/static/img/ai-assistant-inbox.png)

- If **Smart Assistant** (automatic answers) already wrote something, read it carefully. Fix wrong facts or tone before you add your own message.
- If you only get **suggested text** you can edit, treat it like a first draft—not the final word.
- **Always handle yourself** (or with a senior colleague): angry customers, money back, legal topics, health topics, and anything that needs **private account or order details** only staff should see.

How your boss turns these tools on or off: **[AI Assistant](../scaleflow-ai/ai-assistant)**.

## Step 5 — Give the chat to the right person (or back to AI)

At the **top of the chat** you can change who “owns” this conversation next.

![Understand the left categories](/static/img/left-categories-panel.png)

Common actions:

- **Give it to yourself** — You will answer the next message.
- **Give it to a coworker or a team** — Add an **internal note** first: what happened and what you need them to do.
- **Give it back to AI** — Only when your company rules say it is safe, and the customer does not need a human for sensitive topics.

## When you—not the computer—should answer

You should step in and write as a person when:

- The customer is **very upset**.
- It is about **money, refunds, lawyers**, or **private personal or order data**.
- They **ask to speak to a person**.
- The automatic answer is **wrong**, **empty**, or **could upset someone** if sent as-is.
- The job will take **several days or several people** — use a **ticket** so nothing is forgotten.

## When you need a ticket

Use a ticket when the problem is **bigger than one quick message** (lost package, broken product, refund in progress, IT fix, and so on).

1. Create or open a ticket from the **right panel** if your login allows it. How: **[Ticket Usage](./ticket-usage)**.
2. Write the important facts in the ticket **or** in an **internal note** so the next shift does not start from zero.
3. Tell the customer **what will happen next** and **when they can expect news**—only promises your company allows you to make.

Example (your screen may look a little different):

![Ticket created from a conversation](/static/img/ai-create-ticket.png)

## Working well with teammates

1. Before you send a chat to someone else, leave an **internal note**: what you tried and what you need next.
2. Read the **newest messages** before you answer so two people do not say opposite things.
3. If the customer was speaking to you and now someone else will help, your company may want you to **say that kindly in one sentence** in your next Reply.

## How to sound in messages

- Short, clear, polite sentences.
- Say what you understood before you give a long solution.
- Do not promise money back, exact dates, or special deals **unless your boss says you can**.
- One **clear next step** is better than five half steps.

## Story from start to finish (example)

1. A customer writes on Facebook: “I want to cancel my order.”
2. The **automatic assistant** asks for an order number (only if your company turned that on).
3. The customer says they were **charged twice**.
4. The system may **open a ticket** and/or **send the chat to you**.
5. You read the whole chat and check **Tickets** on the right.
6. You add an **internal note** for finance if needed.
7. You **reply** to the customer with the real next step. You **update the ticket** until the problem is closed.

## Good habits

- Clear **Assigned to me** before you take new chats from **Unassigned**, unless your boss uses a different rule.
- Close or finish a chat only when your company’s process says it is done.
- If your company cares about reports, keep **stages and tags** up to date the way they trained you.
